Karen Pendergrass is a standards developer, research strategist, and founder of several pioneering initiatives at the intersection of microbiome science, translational medicine, and regulatory innovation. She is best known for her work in defining microbiome signatures, establishing rigorous methodologies for validating [microbiome-targeted interventions (MBTIs)](https://microbiomemedicine.com/definition/microbiome-targeted-interventions-mbtis/), and developing the emerging field of microbial metallomics in clinical research. Her work is grounded in a systems-level approach that bridges basic microbiological insights with actionable frameworks for clinical practice and industry standards.

### Karen Pendergrass: Founder, Microbiome Signatures

Karen is the founder and architect behind the [Microbiome Medicine Database](https://microbiomemedicine.com), a clinician- and researcher-facing platform designed to formalize disease-associated microbiome patterns through Major Microbial Associations (MMAs) and facilitate intervention development using a structured Microbial Shift and Realignment Process (MSRP). Her validation framework is based on a dual-alignment model, whereby interventions must (1) restore taxa altered in disease-specific microbiome signatures, and (2) yield clinical improvement, thereby co-validating the intervention and the underlying microbial signature.

The database is rapidly evolving into a translational tool that clinicians and researchers can use to guide precision prebiotic, probiotic, phage, dietary, and microbiome-targeted interventions (MBTIs). Karen’s rigorous interpretation of primary literature supports these interventions.

### Pioneering Work in Microbial Metallomics

Karen has led foundational work to integrate trace metal analysis into microbiome research, introducing [microbial metallomics](http://microbialmetallomics.com) as a critical lens to interpret pathogen virulence, microbial selection pressure, and nutrient-immune interactions in chronic diseases. Her metallomic research focuses on the differential acquisition, utilization, and detoxification of trace elements such as nickel, zinc, iron, cadmium, lead, and aluminum among taxa enriched in disease states. Her metallomic modeling has called for [systems-level thinking](https://karenpendergrass.com/2025/11/05/systems-thinking-in-microbiome-research/), yielded novel hypotheses for conditions including [endometriosis](http://microbiomemedicine.com/endometriosis), multiple sclerosis, and polycystic kidney disease, contributing a mechanistic framework to explain microbial persistence and immune evasion in metal-laden tissues.

### Standards Architect and Regulatory Strategist

Karen is also the founder of the [Paleo Foundation](http://paleofoundation.com), where she has spent over 15 years designing and implementing food certification standards for the Paleo, Keto, and Grain-Free Certified markets. These standards, used globally by food manufacturers and brands, reflect a deep understanding of metabolic health, food systems, and compliance frameworks. Karen’s current work extends into the development of the [Heavy Metal Tested and Certified™](http://heavymetaltested.com) program, where she is establishing category-specific contaminant limits, ALARA-based principles, and statistical risk matrices for food, supplements, and personal care products.

### Translation and Critical Thinking

Karen’s scientific approach is marked by a preference for systems-thinking frameworks over formulas. She routinely challenges conventional methodologies that obscure variability in microbiome data interpretation and emphasizes the need for causal inference tools such as the Bradford Hill criteria in clinical microbiome research. Her work aims to address the translational delay between discovery and application— aiming to develop actionable outputs without sacrificing scientific rigor.

###  Personal and Professional Motivation

Karen’s research trajectory was catalyzed by a personal health crisis involving multiple chronic conditions, which she later linked to complex microbiome disruptions and trace metal dysregulation. Her lived experience informs a deep commitment to scientific integrity, patient-centered innovation, and the development of research and regulatory tools that address real-world clinical gaps.

Karen Pendergrass’s work exists at the nexus of microbiome signatures and metallomics research, clinical intervention, and regulatory strategy. Her goal is to accelerate the responsible translation of emerging science into standards, databases, certifications, and frameworks that clinicians, researchers, and industry leaders can trust and operationalize (with the hope of effectuating consumer-driven change in the heavy metal and microbiome medicine landscapes).
